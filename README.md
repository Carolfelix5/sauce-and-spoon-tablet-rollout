#  Sauce & Spoon Tablet Rollout — Project Management Portfolio

## Project Overview

This repository documents the end-to-end project management and 
business analysis work for the **Sauce & Spoon Tabletop Menu Tablet 
Pilot** — a digital transformation initiative for a local restaurant 
chain specializing in scratch-to-table cuisine.

The project involved rolling out tabletop ordering tablets across 
two pilot restaurant locations (North and Downtown) to modernize 
the guest experience, reduce table turn times, increase revenue, 
and improve operational efficiency.

This project was completed as part of my BA/PM portfolio to 
demonstrate real-world project documentation, Agile sprint planning, 
requirements gathering, and data-driven decision making.

##  Documents Included

### 1. Project Proposal
Outlines the business case for implementing tabletop tablets 
across Sauce & Spoon locations. Covers opportunity statement, 
company OKRs, and proposed next steps.

### 2. Project Charter
Defines the full scope, SMART goals, deliverables, stakeholders, 
budget ($50,550 estimated), and key misalignment decisions 
resolved during initiation.

**Key budget breakdown:**
- Hardware & Software: $30,000
- Training Materials: $10,000
- IT Maintenance: $5,000
- Website & Menu Design: $5,000
- Other Customization: $550

### 3. Project Plan — Survey Questions
Documents the customer survey framework used to evaluate 
tablet performance across key indicators including ticket 
time, order accuracy, customer satisfaction, and technical 
issue reporting.

### 4. Test Launch Findings
Results from the 50-customer pilot test launch covering:
- Table wait time data
- Order accuracy rates (72% correct orders)
- Technical issue rates (88% reported issues — led to fixes)
- Customer satisfaction scores
- Recommended next steps

### 5. Impact Report
Post-rollout impact analysis showing:
- Customer satisfaction improvement from 72% (pilot) 
  to 86% (full launch)
- Revenue trend showing 20%+ increase post-rollout
- Food waste reduced by 50%
- Table turn time reduced by 30 minutes
- Key accomplishments and next steps

### 6. Retrospective Review
Structured retrospective capturing what went well and 
what needed improvement from both customer and project 
team perspectives, with evidence and recommended actions.

### 7. Closeout Report
Final project documentation comparing planned vs actual 
outcomes across schedule, cost, and scope. Includes 
lessons learned and next steps for future location rollouts.

##  Jira Project Structure

This project was fully built in Jira as part of the portfolio:

**6 Epics:**
- Tablet Hardware & Software Setup
- Menu & Content Configuration
- Staff Training & Operations
- Pilot Test Launch
- Customer Satisfaction & Feedback
- Full Rollout & Closeout

**18 User Stories** written in standard BA format:
> *"As a [role], I want [feature] so that [benefit]"*

**54+ Subtasks** with defined owners and status tracking

**3 Sprints** planned across June — December 2026:
- Sprint 1: Hardware, POS integration, Pilot launch
- Sprint 2: Content, Training, Operations
- Sprint 3: Feedback analysis, Full rollout, Closeout

**Timeline/Gantt view** showing all 6 Epics across 
a 7-month delivery roadmap.
